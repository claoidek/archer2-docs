MITgcm¶

Warning

The ARCHER2 Service is not yet available. This documentation is in development.

The Maasachusetts Institute of Technology General Circulation Model (MITgcm) is a numerical model designed for study of the atmosphere, ocean, and climate. MITgcm’s flexible non-hydrostatic formulation enables it to simulate fluid phenomena over a wide range of scales; its adjoint capabilities enable it to be applied to sensitivity questions and to parameter and state estimation problems. By employing fluid equation isomorphisms, a single dynamical kernel can be used to simulate flow of both the atmosphere and ocean.
